President of Ukraine Volodymyr Zelensky believes that even long Russian occupation cannot break the morale of brave and united Ukrainians.

The relevant statement was made by President of Ukraine Volodymyr Zelensky on  , posting a short video filmed in the Kharkiv region’s urban-type settlement of Kozacha Lopan, which had recently been liberated from Russian invaders, an Ukrinform correspondent reports.  “Our people are brave and united. Even long occupation can’t break the Ukrainian morale. Courageous head and locals of Kozacha Lopan, a liberated village in the Kharkiv region, have organized food provision for people during occupation. They weren’t afraid to oppose the occupiers and didn’t forget to help each other,” Zelensky wrote.
