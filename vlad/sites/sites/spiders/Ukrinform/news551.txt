Russian troops shelled the Sumy region twice on Sunday, with 52 explosions recorded.

Dmytro Zhyvytskyi, the head of the Sumy Regional Military Administration, said this on  , Ukrinform reports. After 16:30, the Russians struck the Bilopillia community. The village of Volfyne came under fire. The enemy used field artillery. A total of six explosions were recorded. Between 17:45 and 18:00, the invaders began shelling the village with a mortar, with 46 120mm shells hitting the settlement. According to Zhyvytskyi, a local resident born in 1940 was killed in the attack. A local farm was also seriously damaged by enemy fire as about a hundred pigs and a horse were killed. Two grain storage facilities, a water tower, a mill, premises of a pig farm, outbuildings, three wheeled tractors and a ZIL truck were damaged.
