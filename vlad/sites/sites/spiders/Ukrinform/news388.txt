The Russian military killed 21 civilians and injured 31 in Ukraine on Friday, October 7.

Kyrylo Tymoshenko, deputy head of the Office of the President of Ukraine, published such data of regional military administrations on his  channel, Ukrinform reports. In particular, one person was killed and three injured in the Dnipropetrovsk region, 15 people killed (including nine previously killed persons) and 17 injured in the Donetsk region, four people killed and five injured in the Zaporizhzhia region, one person killed in the Sumy region, and six people injured in the Kharkiv region. On October 6, the Russian military killed 22 civilians in Ukraine and wounded 32.
