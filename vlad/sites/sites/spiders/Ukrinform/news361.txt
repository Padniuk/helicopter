Estonia will make a decision next week on a new package of military assistance to Ukraine.

Estonian Prime Minister Kaja Kallas said this on  , Ukrinform reports. "Ukraine has momentum on the battlefield. We must all do more now so it can free its territories. That's why Estonia will decide next week on a new military aid package. We'll give Ukraine more ammunition, winter and individual equipment," Kallas wrote. In relation to the country's GDP per capita, Estonia is among the leaders of countries that help Ukraine. At the beginning of autumn, the Armed Forces of Ukraine received THeMIS unmanned ground vehicle from Estonia for transporting wounded soldiers from the battlefield.
