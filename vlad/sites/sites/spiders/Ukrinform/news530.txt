Russian troops have attacked a civilian car with a tank in the village of Strilecha, Kharkiv district, killing two women.

Oleh Syniehubov, the head of the Kharkiv Regional Military Administration, reported this on  , according to Ukrinform. "In the border village of Strilecha, Kharkiv district, the occupiers fired a tank at a civilian car, killing two women," he wrote. According to him, in the past 24 hours, the enemy massively shelled the Kharkiv and Chuhuiv districts. The Kupiansk and Izium districts also came under enemy fire. Residential and commercial buildings, gas stations, garages and production facilities were destroyed. A five-story residential building in the city of Kupiansk was damaged. "Five civilians were hospitalized with injuries in the Kupiansk district, and three were wounded in the Kharkiv district, including a 91-year-old man. Two people were injured in the Izium district and one in the Chuhuiv district," Syniehubov added.
